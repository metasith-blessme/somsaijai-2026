# Advanced Analytics Plan for SomSaiJai Dashboard

## Objective
To implement advanced, branch-specific analytics on the dashboard, providing deeper insights into product performance, financial liquidity, operational efficiency, and growth trends.

## Key Files & Context
- `3_Automation_Dashboard/index.html`: The main dashboard file where UI and rendering logic live.
- `3_Automation_Dashboard/Sales_System_Automation/config/audit_params.json`: Contains pricing details which are needed for accurate Contribution and Size-Tier calculations.

## Implementation Steps

### 1. Data Augmentation (`index.html` `updateUI` & Parsing)
*   Ensure pricing data from `audit_params.json` (or hardcoded fallbacks like Orange=60, Premium=100, Watermelon=50, Mango=90, Coconut=60, Apple=60) is available to the frontend for calculation.

### 2. Analytics Modules to Build (`index.html` `renderAnalytics`)

*   **Module 1: Product Velocity & Revenue Mix (Bar Chart & Table)**
    *   **Logic:** Calculate total revenue per product (Units * Price) / Total Revenue * 100.
    *   **Display:** A stacked bar chart showing revenue mix per day, plus a summary table showing overall % contribution per SKU for the selected time period.

*   **Module 2: Payment Liquidity Ratio (Gauge/Donut & Metric Card)**
    *   **Logic:** `Total Cash / Total Scan`.
    *   **Display:** A dedicated KPI card showing the ratio (e.g., "1.2x (More Cash)") alongside the existing pie chart, updated to reflect the ratio clearly.

*   **Module 3: Inventory Yield / Burn Rate (Line/Bar Combo Chart)**
    *   **Logic:** `Units Sold / Raw Material Input`. E.g., `(Orange Cups) / (Orange Baskets Used)`.
    *   **Display:** A chart tracking the daily yield (cups per basket/piece) for Orange and Watermelon. This helps identify waste or over-portioning.

*   **Module 4: Net Daily Contribution (Waterfall or Area Chart)**
    *   **Logic:** `Gross Revenue - (Ice + Raw Material Costs)`. Raw material costs require estimating unit cost per raw input based on historical expense data or standard COGS assumptions. *Note: We will need a baseline assumption for COGS (e.g., Orange basket cost) to calculate this dynamically on the frontend if exact daily purchase data isn't mapped 1:1.*
    *   **Display:** A daily trend line showing Gross vs. Contribution Margin.

*   **Module 5: Size-Tier Revenue Density (Pie/Bar Chart)**
    *   **Logic:** Differentiate standard Orange (฿60) vs Premium (฿100) vs standard ฿50 cups (if applicable).
    *   **Display:** Showing revenue generated by tier.

*   **Module 6: Growth Metrics (KPIs)**
    *   **Logic:** Day-over-Day (DoD) and Week-over-Week (WoW) revenue growth.
    *   **Display:** Small indicators on the main KPI cards showing `+X% vs Yesterday`.

### 3. UI/UX Refinements
*   Expand the `Analytics` view to comfortably house these new charts using CSS Grid.
*   Ensure all charts dynamically update when switching between `Branch 1`, `Branch 2`, and `All Branches`.

## Verification & Testing
1.  Verify that toggling branches recalculates all metrics correctly.
2.  Ensure Yield calculates without division-by-zero errors on days with 0 usage.
3.  Validate that Product Mix sums to ~100% of revenue.
